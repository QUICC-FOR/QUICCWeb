QUICC-Base

At continental scale, a large amount of data...

The QUICC-FOR database contains permanent and temporary forest inventory sample plots from United States and Canada. The data has been freely provided by partners and covers 3 eastern Canadian provinces (app. 18,000 plots) and 31 states of eastern USA ( app. 150,000 plots). Data is recorded for seedlings, trees, saplings and stand level. All inventories are geo-referenced. Climatic variables are associated to each plot by interpolating the climatic model ANUSPLIN from Natural Ressources Canada.

At forest stand scale, three permanent plots...

Establishment of these plots has started in 2009 and we will conduct a sampling of growth increment cores to collect fine-scale radial growth data. The three plots are located at the transition between temperate and boreal forests (Estrie, Bas-St-Laurent and Abitibi). The main plot is located at Sutton (Estrie, 20 Ha) along a 200 meters elevation gradient, so that the tree composition shifts from temperate broadleaf at the bottom to coniferous at the top. The other two plots (one near Rimouski further north and the other in Temiscamingue, further north- west) are smaller (5 Ha) and located in homogeneous environmental conditions, but have considerable spatial variability in species composition. Within each of those permanent plots, each tree location (up to 10 cm of DBH) has been recorded.
