What is QUICC-FOR ?

An applied research project on forest ecosystems...

QUICC-FOR is a research project that aims to develop a suite of forest dynamics models to better understand how current land-use practices will affect the diversity, productivity and resilience of forest ecosystems. This modeling suite will be used to provide scenarios of future tree species distribution and productivity at short (10-25 years) and long (100- 200 years) time horizons. We will evaluate the impact of innovative sylvicultural and forest management practices on the response of forest ecosystems to climate change at the transition between temperate and boreal biomes.

The current tools used to predict climate change impact on forest ecosystems are limited because they lack important ecological knowledge. On the one hand, foresters use growth and yield curves parameterized for different regions and climatic conditions to project future productivity. These are inappropriate because they do not consider how the complex ecological interactions between the different tree species will change with novel conditions. On the other hand, the predictions of future species distribution are typically based on the current correlation between observed distribution and several climate descriptors. These models are also inappropriate for forest ecosystems because they do not consider how the complex interplay between dispersal, interspecific interactions and forest management will affect migration dynamics.

This project is unique in linking the best forest ecologists and modelers from Canada, USA and Europe to provide them tools that will be used to assess the impacts and risks of climate change on the productivity of the forests of Eastern Canada. QUICC-FOR is mainly funded by the Strategic program of the Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council of Canada (NSERC) .
